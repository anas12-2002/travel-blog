Flydubai flights to 4 and a new European version starting from 1,500 dirhams

Flydubai announced the launch of four regular flights to four new destinations in Europe, raising the carrier's network in Europe to 43 destinations in 21 countries. Flydubai has become the first Emirati carrier to operate direct flights to the European Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg Airport, starting August 2, 2024, in addition to its direct flights to the Baltic countries, including Riga International Airport in Latvia starting October 11, in addition to Tallinn Airport in Estonia, and Vilnius International Airport in Lithuania, starting next October 12.The new flights will operate from Terminal 3 at Dubai International Airport and will be codeshare flights with Emirates, providing passengers with more options for connecting flights via the international aviation hub in Dubai. Return Business Class fares from Dubai to Basel start at AED 13,000, while Economy Class fares start at AED 1,500, while Business Class fares from Basel to Dubai start at CHF 3,325, and Economy Class fares start at CHF 500. Return Business Class fares from Dubai to Riga start at AED 7,060, and Economy Class fares start at AED 1,920, while Business Class fares from Riga to Dubai start at €1,370, and Economy Class fares start at €380. Business Class return fares from Dubai to Tallinn start at AED 10,400, while Economy Class basic fares start at AED 2,100. Business Class return fares from Tallinn to Dubai start at €2,100, while Economy Class basic fares start at €490. Business Class return fares from Dubai to Vilnius start at AED 7,060, while Economy Class basic fares start at AED 2,180, while Business Class return fares from Vilnius to Dubai start at €1,370, while Economy Class basic fares start at €380.

Emirates Airlines increases the number of its flights to Australia

Emirates has announced that it will expand its operations in Australia by serving its second daily flight between Dubai and Brisbane “EK430/431” with an A380 aircraft starting from October 1, as well as resuming the second daily flight between Dubai and Perth with a Boeing 777-300ER starting from December 1, 2024. Emirates currently serves Australia with 63 weekly flights to Brisbane, Perth, Sydney and Melbourne, using its A380 and Boeing 777 aircraft, carrying 56,000 passengers per week to and from these four major cities. The carrier recently announced doubling the number of Premium Economy seats on its flights to Melbourne starting February 1, and Emirates’ two daily flights to Sydney are also operated with an A380 aircraft in a four-class configuration. Barry Brown, Emirates’ Vice President Commercial Operations Australia, said: “We are delighted to offer more choice and opportunity to Australians travelling from Brisbane and Perth by increasing our operations, as part of our commitment to enhancing global connectivity to our Australian gateways and supporting growth opportunities. This is a testament to the importance of both destinations for tourism and trade, and Emirates’ commitment to ensuring convenient schedules and connections to and from Dubai.” “Whether it’s seamless afternoon connections from Dubai to a wide range of European destinations on our second Perth flight, or a full A380 experience to and from Brisbane to nearly 50 cities served by our flagship aircraft, we provide access to diverse opportunities for business, leisure, culture and more.” Gert-Jan de Graaf, Chief Executive Officer of Brisbane Airport, said: “The second A380 service will enable an additional 100,000 passengers a year to travel between Queensland and Europe, as Emirates is the leading carrier for passengers travelling between Brisbane and Europe.” “More importantly, Emirates is unique in that it is the leading carrier to operate over Moreton Bay, minimising the noise impact on the local community. More than two-thirds of Emirates’ night flights have flown over the bay between July and December 2023,” he added.
